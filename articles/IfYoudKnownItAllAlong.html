Article: If You'd Known the Truth All Along

Have you ever been in a situation where you thought a co-worker had behaved badly towards you, and later you find out it's not true? But you find yourself reluctant to abandon your beliefs, to let go of your resentment, mistrust, or anger.

You may not see why you should let go of those negative feelings. But they're probably making your life harder, in very practical ways. You might be spending time covering your back, dreading meetings, avoiding the person.

Here's how you can stop spending your time and energy on those things, and be more productive at work. Imagine that the new piece of information you got, you had known all along. Think about it. Then ask yourself how you would feel about that person in that case. How you would behave towards them.

Chances are, you wouldn't be spending your time and energy protecting yourself against this person, or avoiding this person. Imagining you had known that new piece of information all along, will probably dissipate most — if not all — of your negative feelings.

Using this technique can help you let go of resentments, rebuild trust, and have a productive working relationship. You'll have more time and energy for your work, and getting promotions, and being happier at work.
