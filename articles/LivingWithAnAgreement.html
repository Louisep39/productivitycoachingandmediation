Living with an Agreement Afterwards

When you're in the middle of a conflict, it's easy to forget about what will happen afterwards. You may be thinking about coming to an agreement to resolve the conflict, but you should also think about how you will live with the agreement in the days, months, and even years to come.

Punishing Someone isn't a Good Idea

In mediation, I often see one person punishing another person. They'll say that if the other person doesn't do something that's been agreed to, then the other person has to do something really big or burdensome — pay a penalty fee, for example. This usually derails the mediation (no surprise there). But also, punishing the other person may make you feel bad later on.

Here's an Example of Why

Let me give you an example. Since mediations are confidential, I'll give you an example about me.

Years ago, I paid some expenses on behalf of someone I was working with. I'll call her Peggy. We agreed on things Peggy could do that would raise the money to pay me back. Then she told me she wasn't going to pay me back, that she hadn't agreed to.

I was angry, and not just about the money. I felt that she'd taken advantage of my generosity. I wanted to sue her in small claims court for the entire amount, even though I knew she wouldn't be able to pay it back all at once.

Instead, I contacted Peggy, and we started talking. We worked out a payment plan she could manage, and she made the payments on time; in fact, she even finished paying it off early.

I've always been happy about this, about how we resolved our dispute. I have to live with myself, and punishing someone isn't according to my values. I would've always regretted it.

You Have to Live with Yourself Afterwards

So next time you are resolving a conflict, make sure you have an agreement that you won't feel bad about later on. You have to live with yourself even after the agreement has been fulfilled.
